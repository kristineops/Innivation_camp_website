The problem:

Due to the corona virus, many children are taken out of school in order not to spread the infection. This is for their own saftey, but can cause several problems when they return to their education. Save the Children has several concerns about childrens return to school, escpecially in rural countries. Some of their concerns are:

  1. Children must return to school when its safe.
  2. We fear many children will not return to school.
  3. We do not have good enough data on why children are out of school.
  4. We do not use the data we have efficiently.
  5. The data do not reach someone that can do something about it.

The solution - How to track attendence:

Radio frequency identifiers - RFIDs. Compared to other technologies such as Bluetooth, biometrics, barcode or the aforementioned web technologies we realized it came out on top on flexibility, simplicity, speed and except for the web technologies and Bluetooth, in distance too!

On top of that, passive RFID tags, which are the ones we are focusing on here today, last virtually forever and when purchased in high volume come at only 1.25 NOK per tag.

An RFID equipped reader that is capable of handling the data we are after, such a terminal does vary wildly in price, we have found them ranging between as little as 5000 and as much as 200000 NOK depending on diverse specifications. Even if we focused on the most expensive few of the bunch, they still come up on top when compared to other systems. Their batteries can either be recharged using simple solar panels, interchangeable batteries or by periodically recovering the terminals to gather the accumulated data, recharging the batteries, and delivering them back.

Another benefit is that thanks to the range we can ensure that the technologies aren´t salvaged for materials by encasing them if necessary. The nature of RFID tags allow them to hold up to 32kb. That´s enough to hold the entire Declaration of the United States of America around five times and a half. Needless to say your benefit is that you can ensure you obtain data from name, age, gender, and anything else you could need from the user. But how to do that?

The best way implementing this idea was by using reusable and recyclable rubber bands that come with an adjustable strap so that it can be passed on onto any student of any size and age. The rubber bands are durable and contain the RFID tag, an ID number and are also grey, so that they are gender neutral and can contrast with the environment in case of loss.

Since the terminal holds data of attendance and lack thereof as well as the students information, both instructed teachers and Save the Children can benefit from obtaining all sorts of data that they can use to make sure their efforts are being focused on the right spot.
